Moving About In Space

Every action you take takes time, which is measured in turns and subturns. There are 100 subturns in a turn, and after each turn the enemy agents move to another sector. Many actions also run some risk of attracting suspicion and increasing your "heat" level. These actions are always modified by the Federation influence of the Planet or System you're currently at, but also can be modified by other factors as well. Here is a table of some common actions you can take and how many subturns they require, as well as how much risk they pose of Federal Detection:

Action Subturns Heat Risk Notes
Warping to another system (via wormhole) 36 minor
Emergency Warp 8 none
Travelling to (and orbiting) a Planet 50 negligible modified by ship speed
Landing on a Planet 36 significant mmodified by DNA scrambling
Taking off from a Planet 16 negligible
Planetary Activities 24 moderate modified by DNA scrambling
Energy Scooping 72 none attracts pirates if done repeatedly
Piracy 100 severe

The speed of your ship also determines your chances of evading a Federal Agent if you should encounter one in open space.

Obtaining Fuel

Fuel may be obtained by scooping at Stars, recharging on planets which offer that service, and also via piracy. When scooping, the type of star as well and the level of your photonic convertor determines how much fuel each scoop yields. Here is a chart of the various star types:

Star Class Color Yield
O Blue 100%
B Blueish White 75%
A White 60%
F Yellowish White 50%
G Yellow 36%
K Light Orange 25%
M Light Orangeish Red 12%

Emergency Warps

You start the game with two emergency warp devices, which, when activated, will materialize you and your ship in a random system somewhere in the galaxy. Emergency warping costs 72 units of energy to operate, and if you activate it with less than the required amount your ship may take significant damage and possibly explode.

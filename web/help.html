SpaceFugue Help

The Story So Far

You were the captain of the INFOAWAP (Intergalactic Federation Of Aligned Worlds And Protectorates) Excelsior, arguably the most advanced starship in galactic history. Now you're just a fugitive, running from the system you had spent your life defending, currently flying solo in a tiny run-down barge at the edges of charted interstellar civilization. Your mission: find the secret supercomputer somewhere in Federation Space, hack it, and return the data to the Federation Homeworld. This data will prove to the Galactic High Council what you have recently discovered: that the Federation has been seriously compromised by a reclusive civilization unknown to all but a tiny few - the Krakkar, whose nefarious goal is to create the first transgalactic antimatter wormhole in order to fullfill the prophecy of their ancient religion, a prophecy that would wreak devestation and ruin throughout the cosmos.

How to Play

  • Your mission is to find the Federation Supercomputer "Star One", broadcast the details as many times as you can at Heavy-Density Commerical Worlds, and then arrive at the Federation Homeworld Xaxle in the Mentos System. While doing so, you must avoid the three Federation Agents searching for you and also defeat any pirates which may attack you.
  • Most actions you take consume a certain number of "subturns", and there are 100 subturns in a turn. Each turn the Federation agents searching for you move to another system and the chance of that system being in your direction is determined by your "heat" index which ranges from 0-100.
  • The Galaxy is connected by warp lanes from each traversable star system. Each trip consumes energy from your ship's hyperspace engine and initially takes 36 subturns to complete.
  • You can replenish energy at each system by selecting "Fuel Scoop", which recharges your batteries at a rate corresponding to the system's star type, with blue being the most efficient and yellow/orange being the least. Each scoop takes 72 subturns to complete, and subsequent scoops in the same system could risk attracting pirates.
  • Systems have a Federation and a Tech Level, indicating respectively their degree of loyalty to the Federation and how advanced their technology is, which in turn influences the Federation and Technology levels of the planets within each system.

Planets

Most systems contain planets you can land at with fed/tech levels related to that of the system they are in. They also have three initially unknown attributes, their residential/commercial/industrial densities which determine what actions you can take on each planet. Most planetary activities require 24 subturns to complete. Your first activity at any planet requires flying over to it which requires energy from your ship's sub-light drive and consumes roughly half a turn (depending on your ship's speed).

Planetary Actions:
  • Light Residential: Scout -> This reveals the RCI densities of all planets in the system as well as various nearby systems (determined by tech level).
  • Medium Residential: Hack: -> This reveals how many jumps away you are from Star One and what initial direction to take to get there.
  • Heavy Residential: Spy -> Indicates where the three Federation Agents currently are, duration related to tech level.
  • Light Commercial: Shoplift -> Steal credits, amount is correlated to tech level, punishment (if caught) is related to fed level.
  • Medium Commercial: Trade Mission -> Gain insider knowledge of where best to sell certain goods. Reward correlates to your ship's cargo capacity.
  • Heavy Commercial: Broadcast -> Once you know were Star One is, you can broadcast its contents to the rest of the galaxy, which will increase your chances of success upon landing at Xaxle.
  • Light Industrial: Repair/Recharge ship -> hopefully self-evident.
  • Medium Industrial: Ship Modification -> see the "Ship Modification" section for details.
  • Heavy Industrial: BioHacking -> Each action you take has a certain chance of drawing unwanted attention and increasing your "heat" level. The more "scrambled" your DNA is the less likely this will occur (though no guarantees about how many fingers you'll wind up with).

Pirates

  • Each time you travel to a new planet or perform an "extra" fuel scoop, you run the risk of a pirate encounter.
  • The fed level of the planet (or system in case of fuel scooping) determines the liklihood of an attack and its strength.
  • Your shields will block damage until depleted, at which point your hull will be damaged. If your hull reaches zero, your ship is destroyed.

Ship Modifications

  • Thrusters: inversely determines how many subturns are required when travelling to a planet
  • Lasers: how much damage your ship can inflict upon its adversaries
  • Shields: how much damage your ship's shields can withstand
  • Hull Integrity: how much damage your ship's hull can withstand
  • Cargo Capacity: how much cargo your ship can ferry (determines trade mission rewards)
  • Energy Capacity: how much energy your ship's batteries can store
  • Photonic Convertor: how much energy you can extract from fuel scooping
  • Emergency Warps: how many times you can perform an emergency warp.
